FAQs

Is Match really free to use?

Yes, there are no sign-up fees. Match Exchange takes no commission on any successful purchase that results from parties being introduced through Match. Match is merely a free tool for buyers and sellers of agricultural commodities to have more options to do business with.

What is a Scout?

You create a Scout to tell the market what you are planning to buy or sell and within which area. Match Exchange’s algorithm with display the available counter-parties interested in the same commodity.

What is a Lot?

You create a Lot to tell the market more details about the commodity that you want to buy or sell. These details are the 5 P’s (product, price, period of availability, payment terms and position) and 2 Q’s (quality and quantity). You can create multiple Lots to partially market your produce.

Will my Scouts or Lots be removed or disappear after some time?

No, your Scouts and Lots will remain on Match, for as long as you are buying or selling the commodity for which the Scout or Lot has been created. You can delete a Scout or a Lot from the “Scouts” or the “Lots” screens if you no longer want a specific Scout or Lot to be matched with another user’s Scouts or Lots.

Is there any obligation to transact with the matching counterparty?

No, after you have a match, you and the counterparty are introduced to each other. If you are unsure about making a transaction with a specific party, you are welcome to decline the offer and recreate your lot on Match.

How do I know that the counterparty that I negotiate with is a trusted person or business?

Kindly see the “Know your counterparty” page in the app for helpful guidelines to verify the counterparty. These guidelines will also be sent to you in an email once a negotiation has been completed. Match Exchange will also verify trusted users in the near future.
