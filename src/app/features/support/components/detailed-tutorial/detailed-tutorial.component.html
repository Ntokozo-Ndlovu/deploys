Detailed Tutorial

So How Does It Work?

Match Exchange stimulates a cash market at every point where a Lot is created, and doing so creates a transparent and fair market for both buyers and sellers.

Follow the four easy steps to get the perfect Match for your produce.

Step 1: Create a Scout

Tell the Market what you are planning to buy or sell. You can indicate what products you plan to produce or purchase, and the Scout will display how many counterparties are currently available with matching needs.

• Product (commodity): e.g. Wheat
• Position (location): e.g. Malmesbury

Step 2: Create a Lot

Match Exchange stimulates a cash market at every point where a Lot is created. By creating a Match Exchange Lot you are communicating to the Market that you are a willing buyer or seller of a specific portion of your product.

Step 3: Compare Matches

Once you have created a Lot, Match Exchange will match your Lot with counter Lots of similar needs. Counter Lots display the 5 P’s and 2 Q’s of your own Lot in comparison to the counter Lots, and you can enter into a negotiation with the matching Lot of your choosing.

Step 4: Negotiate

Both the buyer and seller are notified when a negotiation on a Lot has started. In the Negotiating Room the buyer and seller can obtain consensus on the 5 P's and 2 Q's. When consensus has been reached, you have a Match!

Step 5: Match

Once you have a Match you will be introduced to the counterparty with the 5 P’s & 2 Q’s you agreed upon.

Match Exchange only facilitates the platform for getting consensus on the marketing of your product. It is up to the buyer and seller to follow through on the transaction from here via further communication.

Step 6: Know your counterparty (“KYC-party”)

Refer to the “Know your Counterparty ("KYC-party")” section for steps to ensure that the counterparty you are dealing with is a trusted party.

Match Exchange is currently implementing ‘user verification’ to verify users on the platform. This will entail proof of identity, ownership, and authority to transact on behalf of the entity.

Match Exchange is also implementing a ‘user experience rating’ system to rate the experience between the buyer and seller after a successful match.
